# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SkyMate serves smart-glasses users who are outdoors, walking, commuting, or deciding whether to step outside for stargazing. They use voice first, glance at a compact AIUI card, and need a fast answer without reading a dense weather or astronomy report.

## Product Purpose

SkyMate helps users decide whether now or tonight is worth stargazing, what they can likely see, and where to look. Success means the user gets a short spoken conclusion, sees 2 to 4 useful sky targets, and can continue into a focused detail view for one object without leaving the conversation.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, companionable. SkyMate should feel like a knowledgeable friend standing beside the user under the sky: direct enough for voice interaction, gentle enough for uncertainty, and visually quiet enough for a small glasses display.

## Anti-references

SkyMate should not look like a dense weather dashboard, a scientific report, a generic chatbot landing page, or a decorative astronomy poster. Avoid complex tables, long explanations, hard-to-read star maps, excessive purple/blue space gradients, and UI that assumes the user is looking at a phone-sized screen for a long time.

## Design Principles

1. Conclusion first: the card should surface the recommendation before supporting details.
2. Glanceable by default: every state should be readable in a few seconds on a small display.
3. Voice and UI stay together: the interface should extend the conversation, not feel like a separate app.
4. Show only the next useful choice: tool results should become a small set of star buttons, not a full catalog.
5. Uncertainty stays honest: cloudy weather, missing location, or imprecise sky positions should be expressed naturally.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prioritize high contrast, short copy, large tap targets, and reduced cognitive load for hands-free smart-glasses use. Motion should be minimal and state-driven. Do not rely on color alone to identify object type; include text labels such as 月亮、行星、亮星、星座.
